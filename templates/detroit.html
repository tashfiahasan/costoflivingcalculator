Detroit

Detroit Institute of Arts

Its permanent collection includes more than 65,000 works of art within more than 100 galleries. This includes pieces from Africa, Oceania, and the Indigenous Americas; art of the Near East and classical antiquity; collections from medieval Europe; and American art and culture.

Motown Museum

Also referred to as "Hitsville USA" for its iconic sign, the Motown Museum is a small shingle-clad building that was occupied from 1957 to 1972 by the studio where records of the "Motown sound" were produced.

The Ford Piquette Avenue Plant

The building is a National Historic Landmark, formerly the center of manufacturing for what was once the world's most popular car.
