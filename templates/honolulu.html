USS Arizona Memorial

This was built in 1962 to honor the 2,335 sailors, soldiers, and Marines, as well as the 68 civilians, who lost their lives during the attack on Pearl Harbor.

Diamond Head

A volcanic crater that’s been dormant for more than 150,000 years, locals call Diamond Head “Lēʻahi,” which means “brow of the ʻahi” and “cape of fire.”

Ala Moana Center

The mall features live music, extensive food court options, a cultural center where you can learn hula and discover other cultural activities, and is home to Shirokiya Japan Village Walk, a food hall built like a small Japanese temple town, offering more than 30 authentic Japanese food vendors.
